Linus Torvalds

The Creator of linux

Biography

Linus Benedict Torvalds was born on 28 December 1969. He is a Finnish software engineer who is the creator and, historically, the lead developer of the Linux kernel, used by Linux distributions and other operating systems such as Android. He also created the distributed version control system Git.

Achievements

  • 1991 - Created the linux Terminal and the version control system Git.
  • 1996 - In 1996, the asteroid 9793 Torvalds was named after him.
  • 2004 - Created the linux Terminal and the version control system Git.
  • 2006 - The magazine's Europe edition named him one of the revolutionary heroes of the past 60 years.
  • 2010 - The Britannica Guide to the World's Most Influential People, Torvalds was listed among The 100 Most Influential Inventors of All Time
  • 2010 - Awarded by the Takeda foundation
